Content Management

Generating content is not enough. You also need to manage it. From drafts, editing, and scheduling to publishing status, this module brings all SEO content into one place so website updates become clearer, more scalable, and easier to sustain over time.

Common Pain Points

  • You generate articles but have no clear place to manage them
  • You have too many drafts but no clear status or structure
  • Your content publishing rhythm is inconsistent and easy to interrupt
  • You want scheduled publishing, but the workflow feels messy
  • As the number of articles grows, content becomes harder to organize and track

What does this module do?

The content management module organizes AI-generated articles in one place so each piece of content has a clear status and next step.

It does more than simply store articles. It creates a full content lifecycle, making the path from generation to publishing more structured and manageable.

  • Draft creation
  • Manual editing
  • Scheduled publishing
  • Live publishing
  • Ongoing tracking and optimization

Core Features

  • View all articles in one place
  • Separate draft, scheduled, and published states
  • Edit and update content easily
  • Connect with your topic bank and AI generation workflow
  • Create a more structured and trackable publishing rhythm

How It Works

  1. Select a topic from your topic bank
  2. Generate an article with AI
  3. Review the draft inside content management
  4. Edit the article and SEO fields
  5. Choose whether to publish immediately or schedule it

What do you gain?

  • More centralized and organized content operations
  • Less confusion after AI content generation
  • A more stable publishing rhythm
  • A clearer path from generation to live publishing
  • A website that gradually builds content into long-term assets

Why is content management important?

Without content management, AI-generated articles often stop at the “generated” stage. But the real value comes when content can be edited, published, accumulated, and turned into lasting search entry points and traffic assets.

With content management in place, articles are no longer temporary pieces of text. They become assets that can be published, improved, and expanded over time.
